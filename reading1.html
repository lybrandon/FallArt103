Q2. -- For the 4 system definations you were to look up please give in your own words what they mean and give an example of them for each case. Modularity : The use of standardized units that physically connect to each other to form larger compositions. A good example of this is a brick building. The entire building is made up of symmetrical bricks, stacked on top of each other to form a much larger object. If there was a problem with the structure, the bricks could be reconfigured, removed/added when needed. Decomposibility: This is the ability to be broken down into smaller components or into something that shares the same molecular structure it had before. A good example of this could be a banana. The peel will begin to change form and shape once it is exposed to air. When the banana decomposes, it is in a completely different state. Emergence: Is the appearance of patterns or intelligence appearing from the interactions of components at a granular level. A good example of this is human consciousness. There are specific ideas that are triggered by muliptiple understandings in your brain to help see something that may be unclear at first. Chaos Theory: This is a scientific theory that studies a branch of mathematics that were thought to believe to have completely random states of disorder yet still provide features of self familiarity and organization. An example of this is the butterfly effect. This is where a miniscule event could result is something with enormous proportion.
